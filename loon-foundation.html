The Loon Foundation

"The Loon Foundation was founded in 2002 as the Ruby Lake Lagoon Nature Reserve Society", and since that time, "our scope has expanded well beyond the Ruby Lake Lagoon, and in 2020 we changed our name to The Loon Foundation to better represent our wide reaching efforts to conserve biodiversity and foster environmental stewardship." In addition to community science and education, and in collaboration with the shíshálh Nation, this organization is leading the build of the Pender Harbour Ocean Discovery Station. (Quotes from the Foundation's website.)

Biomonitoring: Yes, there are several biomonitoring and community science programs.

  • Forage fish
  • Seagrass
  • Invasive species
  • Pinnipeds
  • Salmonid escapement
  • Juvenile escapement
  • Larval crabs
  • Coastal water nearshore birds
  • Loons

Data from these programs are typically shared with government agencies and NGOs.

The Foundation also organizes an annual BioBlitz, and findings are recorded on iNaturalist.

Education: Yes. Occasional educational gatherings and a summer camp for children typically occur in any given year. See the website for upcoming events.

Biomonitoring and Community Science Volunteer Opportunities: Yes. Contact the Society to determine current opportunities.

In addition to the monitoring programs, the Foundation is engaged in several other activities aimed at enhancing biodiversity and its appreciation on the coast, and there are volunteer opportunities in these other programs.

Data Repository BioMonitoring Methods

Biomonitoring Data

Forage fish: This program is part of the Coastal Forage Fish Network. These data are deposited with the Pacific Salmon Foundation and can be accessed at https://marinedata.psf.ca/knowledge-hubs/forage-fish/

Salmon Escapement Monitoring: Data shared with Fisheries and Oceans Canada. These data appear in the periodic South Coast Salmon Strait of Georgia Stock Assessment.

Seagrass Monitoring: Data not currently shared.

Rocky Intertidal: Data not currently shared.

Pinniped monitoring: Data not currently shared.

Coastal Waterbirds: Data shared with Bird Studies Canada's Nature Counts and eBird.

Loon Monitoring: Data shared with Bird Studies Canada Loon Lakes Monitoring Program.

Larval Crab Monitoring: Data shared with the Hakai Institute's Sentinels of Change program and the Pacific Northwest Crab Research Group.

Green Crab Monitoring: Data shared with Fisheries and Oceans Canada.

Anderson Creek Water Quality: Data not currently shared.
