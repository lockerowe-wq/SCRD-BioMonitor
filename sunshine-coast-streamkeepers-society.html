Sunshine Coast Streamkeepers Society

"Sunshine Coast Streamkeepers Society is a group of people from different communities along the Sunshine Coast. Our goal is to have each creek monitored and to advocate for wild salmon!" (Quoted from the Society's website.)

Biomonitoring Programs:

  • Recording creek water and air temperatures
  • Spawning counts

Data from this program is shared with relevant agencies.

Education Programs: No current programs.

Biomonitoring and Community Science Volunteer Opportunities: Yes. Contact the Society to determine current opportunities.

In addition to monitoring streams, the Society is engaged in several other activities aimed at enhancing stream biodiversity and its appreciation, and there are volunteer opportunities in these programs.

Website: https://sunshinecoaststreamkeepers.com/
Location: Largely centred around Gibsons and Roberts Creek
Contact: scstreamkeepers@gmail.com

Biomonitoring Data

Stream temperature: These data are deposited with Pacific DataStream (https://pacificdatastream.ca/) and can be accessed here: https://datastream.org/en-ca/dataset/aed02d78-47ab-48f5-920d-0de8f8b55a0f

Salmon streams: These data can be found on the Society's website: https://sunshinecoaststreamkeepers.com/spawning-counts-for-creeks/. These data are also deposited in the Pacific Streamkeepers Federation's database and can be accessed here: https://www.streamkeepers.info/profile.php?user=3035
