Sargeant Bay Society

"Established in 1978, the Sargeant Bay Society is a volunteer non-profit organization located on the Sunshine Coast of British Columbia, Canada. We work to support, enhance, and protect the ecologically sensitive habitats found within Sargeant Bay, its watershed, and neighbouring wetlands." (Quoted from the Society's website.)

Biomonitoring Programs: Yes.

  • Colvin Creek spawning counts by Sargeant Bay's Streamkeepers.

Education Programs: Yes. Occasional educational gatherings and nature walks occur in any given year. See the website for upcoming events.

Biomonitoring and Community Science Volunteer Opportunities: Yes. Contact the Society to determine current opportunities.

In addition to monitoring Colvin Creek salmon, the Society is engaged in several other activities aimed at enhancing biodiversity and its appreciation at Sargeant Bay and nearby watersheds, and there are volunteer opportunities in these other programs.

Website: https://sargbay.ca/
Location: Centred around Halfmoon Bay and Smuggler Cove and Marine Park
