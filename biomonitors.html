On Being a Biomonitor

Getting Involved

There are many ways to contribute to biomonitoring on the Sunshine Coast.

Volunteer

The non-profit organizations featured on this website offer a variety of volunteer opportunities for community members to contribute to biodiversity monitoring. Whether you're a seasoned naturalist or just starting out, young or not so young, there are likely programs suited to your interests and experience level. Reach out to any of the organizations featured on this site to find out how you might get involved.

Record your Observations

You can also contribute by engaging on your own with easy to use web-based platforms, such as eBird and iNaturalist, where you can record your observation of species you've seen at home or any place you might be on the Coast.

Join or Donate

Finally, you can contribute by joining or donating to one or more of the non-profits featured here.

Impact

In addition to the fun of engaging with biodiversity and like-minded people, your observations will contribute to our understanding of the state of biodiversity on the Coast, and even to new policy and formal research in ecology and evolution. Some of these impacts will be shared on the Research Stories page.

Community Biomonitors

Here we will feature some of the Sunshine Coast community members leading or volunteering in some of the local biomonitoring programs. More to come!
